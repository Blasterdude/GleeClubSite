A Little Bit About Us

The WPI Men's Glee Club is the oldest student organization on campus. Its deep traditions of singing and brotherhood go back over 140 years and continue on today with our current chorus of over 60 members.

We perform from a repertoire of extensive arrangements, including works of Mozart, Eric Whitacre Schubert, Paul Winter, and Stravinsky. We frequently perform alongside Alden Voices, WPI's Women's Chior, as well as groups from all over New England. Traditionally this has involved Smith College, Wells College, Radcliffe College, Regis College, Mount Holyoke College, Wellesley College, Bosten University, and many others. Performances are also conducted with area orchestras, including the Concord Symphony and New England Philharmonic. The Glee Club frequently tours domestically and internationally. Past tours include New York City, Montreal, Greece, Ireland, Italy, Spain, and London.

The Glee Club is currently under the direction of Professor John Delorey, a professor of music and theory at WPI.


While not required, WPI students can participate in Glee Club for 1/6th academic credit each semester.


As a non-auditioned group, anyone is openly welcomed to join our longstanding traditions of brotherhood and song. If you enjoy singing and want to make lifelong friends and memories, this is the place for you.

For those looking to hone their musical skills, we are also associated with Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM), one of WPI's many acapella groups. You can learn more about them here.

If you're interested in taking Glee Club for credit, please visit Bannerweb.

Where To Find Us

All of our rehearsals are held at First Baptist Church, located next to the Park Avenue parking garage, just down the steps by the Recreation Center. Rehearsals are held at the following times:

Tuesdays from 5:00-6:30 PM

Thursdays from 5:00-6:30 PM

On Tuesday nights we rehearse by ourselves, but on Thursdays we rehearse alongside Alden Voices (WPI's Women's Choir). Together we are known as The WPI Festival Chior, and often sign in off-campus concerts.

Additionally, many of our (completely optional, but strongly encouraged and totally fun) social events take place after rehearsals on Tuesday nights, so keep that in mind when planning your schedule.
